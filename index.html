About

I am a senior Ph.D. research scholar in Computer Science & Engineering at IIIT Hyderabad. I previously completed my M.Tech. in Cryptology & Security from the Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata, and hold a Master's and Bachelor's degree in Mathematics from the Banaras Hindu University and University of Calcutta, respectively. My research focuses on hybrid quantum-classical communication protocols-things like authentication, digital signatures, and access control schemes that are not just theoretically secure, but actually deployable in real-world systems. I'm interested in closing the gap between elegant cryptographic ideas and the messy constraints of practice. Alongside this, I explore the intersection of quantum computing and machine learning, where new computational models open up different ways of learning, optimization, and decision-making. More broadly, I enjoy working on problems that sit at the boundary of security, data, and emerging technologies, where small design choices can have large, real-world consequences.
